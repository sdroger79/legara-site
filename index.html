The Legara Model
A workforce platform with operational infrastructure built in.

Legara is not a staffing agency that places a clinician and walks away. Every provider is supported by dedicated operational staff, every health center has a named relationship owner, and every encounter flows through a compliance architecture designed for FQHC regulatory requirements.

01

Clinician Network

Independent behavioral health providers sourced, vetted, and credentialed by Legara. CA-licensed, experienced in FQHC environments, matched to your patient population. Therapy and prescriber coverage, with bilingual and culturally competent providers available. Every clinician is selected for FQHC readiness, not just licensure. They understand the patient population, the compliance environment, and what it means to operate inside a safety-net system.

02

Operational Infrastructure

Every provider is backed by a dedicated Patient Service Representative handling scheduling, patient coordination, and documentation. Every health center has a Partner Relationship Owner as their single point of contact. This is why utilization is 82% and turnover is under 3%. Your front desk doesn't take on additional scheduling volume. Legara's PSRs are trained and measured on provider productivity, so panels fill faster and stay full.

03

Encounter-Based Economics

You pay a flat rate per completed patient encounter. No salary, no benefits, no recruiter fees, no cost until encounters begin. Your health center retains full clinical authority, full billing control, and full compliance oversight. The model means your behavioral health costs scale directly with patient volume. When demand dips, your costs dip with it. When panels are full, every encounter generates revenue.

The Challenge
The math behind behavioral health hiring doesn't work.

FQHCs budget $135K for a therapist. The real number, after benefits, support staff, recruiter fees, and months of ramp time, is closer to $229K. One in three of those hires leaves within the first year. And during the entire recruitment and onboarding cycle, patients wait.

Why Legara
Purpose-built operational infrastructure for FQHC behavioral health.

Most approaches to the behavioral health workforce shortage address one piece of the problem. Staffing agencies place a clinician. Telehealth companies connect a screen. Neither builds the operational layer that determines whether providers succeed and stay. Legara built that layer.

Dedicated Support for Every Provider

Each clinician is backed by a full-time Patient Service Representative handling no more than 3 to 4 providers. Scheduling, documentation, patient coordination. All handled. This ratio was validated empirically: when we loosened it, productivity dropped immediately. When we restored it, productivity returned.

A Named Relationship Owner for Your Health Center

Your Partner Relationship Owner serves 2 to 5 FQHCs and coordinates directly with your BH Director. One call, one person, one answer.

Compliance by Architecture, Not by Promise

Three-entity structure designed around California's corporate practice of medicine law. Your health center retains full clinical authority. Legara never exercises clinical control. Including daily claims scrubbing and monthly quality reviews on a minimum 3% of encounters.

Economics That Align With Your Mission

Per-encounter pricing. You pay only for completed patient encounters. No salary, no benefits, no FTE risk. Zero cost until encounters begin.
