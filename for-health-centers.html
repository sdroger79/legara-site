The Reality
You need more behavioral health capacity. Hiring alone can't get you there.

Your patients are waiting months for care. Your board is asking about behavioral health access. Your clinical leadership wants to expand — but hiring takes time, and the numbers on a new FTE don't pencil out until the panel is full. That gap can take months you can't afford to absorb.

Your employed staff is the foundation. But the clinician market is tight — bilingual, culturally competent behavioral health providers with FQHC experience don't come off a job board. And even when you find one, there's the salary commitment, benefits load, credentialing timeline, and the risk that they don't work out. Legara fills the capacity gap your team can't cover on its own.

And here's what doesn't show up in the budget forecast: salaried behavioral health providers average a 25% no-show rate — significantly higher than primary care. That's a quarter of their scheduled time you're paying for with zero return. They have no financial incentive to fill empty slots, and neither does your scheduling team. The true cost of an employed provider is far higher than the salary line suggests.

The Real Comparison
What does behavioral health capacity actually cost?

At first glance, the per-encounter cost of a Legara clinician may appear higher than a salaried provider. But that comparison uses the wrong denominator. A salaried provider's cost per completed encounter is far higher than it looks — because a significant share of their paid time never produces a billable visit.

Start with clinical availability: employed providers are typically seeing patients only about 70% of their paid hours. The rest is consumed by staff meetings, mandatory trainings, supervision, committee work, and other organizational obligations that come with being on payroll. Then layer in no-shows — 25% of their scheduled appointments result in a patient who doesn't appear. The math: 70% clinical availability × 75% show rate = roughly 52% effective utilization. You're paying a full salary for a provider who's seeing patients barely half the time.

Legara clinicians don't carry organizational overhead. They show up to see patients — that's the job. Our scheduling staff is held to a 95% utilization benchmark, and our clinicians are incentivized to keep their panels full. Our no-show rate runs at 14%. The math: 95% clinical availability × 86% show rate = roughly 82% effective utilization. That's a 30-point gap — and you only pay for the completed encounters.

Not Telehealth-as-Usual
You've seen telehealth before. This is something different.

Traditional telehealth vendors employ or subcontract clinicians, bill you hourly or per-session, and drop a provider into your system with minimal integration. They weren't built for FQHCs — they were built for commercial health systems and adapted down. The result: poor EHR integration, no scheduling support, no understanding of FQHC compliance requirements, and a financial model that doesn't align with encounter-based revenue.

Legara isn't a telehealth company that also serves FQHCs. It's a workforce platform built from the ground up for safety-net healthcare — with the operational infrastructure, incentive alignment, and FQHC-specific expertise that generic telehealth vendors don't offer.
